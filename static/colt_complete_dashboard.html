COLT Dataset - Comprehensive Diplomatic Travel Analysis Dashboard

About the Dataset

This dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of the Country and Organization Leader Travel (COLT) dataset from the Frederick S. Pardee Institute for International Futures at the University of Denver. The dataset tracks diplomatic travel of heads of government and state from 1990 to 2024, offering insights into global diplomatic relations, patterns, and trends.

Dashboard Contents

Static Visualizations

These visualizations provide a comprehensive overview of diplomatic travel patterns from the COLT dataset.

Diplomatic Trips Over Time

This visualization shows the number of diplomatic trips taken by heads of government each year from 1990 to 2024.

Top Destinations

This chart displays the top 10 most visited countries by heads of government.

Regional Distribution

This pie chart shows the distribution of diplomatic visits across different regions of the world.

Trip Duration

This histogram displays the distribution of diplomatic trip durations, with the mean and median highlighted.

Top Leaders

This chart shows the top 15 most traveled heads of government in the dataset.

Travel Between Regions

This heatmap shows the flow of diplomatic travel between different regions, highlighting the most active regional relationships.

Comprehensive Trips Visualization

This visualization shows the top 15 countries in three categories: most visited countries, countries with the most diplomatic trips, and countries with the most diverse destinations. Use the dropdown menus to select categories and countries.

Country Pair Analysis

This visualization allows you to analyze diplomatic visits between specific country pairs over time. Use the dropdowns to select visiting and visited countries. You can select any combination of countries to see their diplomatic relationship over time.

Leader Timeline Visualization

This visualization shows the diplomatic activity of the top 15 leaders over time. Use the dropdown to select specific leaders.

Diplomatic Diversity Visualization

This bubble chart visualization shows the diversity of diplomatic travel for top countries, with bubble size representing total trips and color representing the number of unique destinations visited.

Key Findings

  • The overall number of diplomatic trips has generally increased from 1990 to 2024, with notable fluctuations during global events.
  • European countries dominate both as visitors and destinations, highlighting the density of diplomatic relations within Europe.
  • Some countries show clear patterns of regional focus, while others display more global diplomatic engagement.
  • Top leaders from major powers demonstrate the most consistent diplomatic travel activity over time.
  • The United States, China, and major European powers are among the most frequently visited destinations.
  • International organizations like the UN show distinctive travel patterns compared to individual countries.

Data Source: Country and Organization Leader Travel (COLT) dataset
